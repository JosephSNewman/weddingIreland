Greetings and salutations, esteemed and noble guests,

King Anderson and Queen Gaylene of House Page

'Tis with great joy and merriment that we welcome ye to our wedding festivities. Mayhap your journey hither be long and arduous, but we trust that ye find respite and mirth in good company upon arrival.

We take this occasion to extend our gratitude for your presence, and we hope that this day shall live forever in your hearts as a memory to cherish. As a token of our appreciation, we have gathered some gifts to aid ye in your travels, and to impart a sense of the beauty and magic of Ireland that awaits ye.

We have included some offerings from the trade establishment of Joseph, as well as other trinkets and treasures, that we trust shall bestow upon ye fortune and comfort in your voyage.

May the road rise up to meet ye, and the wind be always at your back, as ye journey forth. We eagerly look forward to creating more wondrous and joyous memories with ye, and thank ye for gracing us with your presence in our lives.

With warm regards and deepest affection,
